You created a great app and put it on the App Store. And now you wait. And wait. And nothing happens. Why? Because there are millions of apps in the stores, and finding yours is like looking for a needle in a haystack. The solution is connecting it with the web.

## The Web as the Main Source of Installs
Most people don't look for a solution to a problem in the App Store, but on Google. "How to lose weight", "Best navigation", "Dinner recipes". If you have a website with quality content (SEO) that answers these questions, you can convert visitors into app users.

## Landing Page for the App
Every app needs its "home page" on the web.
- **Explanation of Features:** You have more space on the web than in the store description.
- **Reviews and Proof:** Show satisfied users.
- **Clear Buttons:** "Download on the App Store" and "Google Play".

## Deep Linking: The Magic Connection
**Deep Linking** technology allows a click on a link on the web to open a specific screen directly in your app (if the user has it installed) or send them to download it (if they don't).
Example: You send an email with an offer. The customer clicks on mobile and the product opens directly in the app, where they have their card saved and buy with one click.

## Measurement and Optimization
Connecting the web and app gives you a complete picture. You know that a user came from an article about "weight loss", downloaded the app, and bought a premium plan after a week. This data is gold for marketing.

Don't rely on chance. Use the power of the web to grow your app.
